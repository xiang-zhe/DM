              <p class="speakable">Just when hopeless romantics thought that mind-boggling dating trends like <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/2017/09/28/submarining-is-latest-dating-woe-to-baffle-romantics.html" target="_blank">submarining</a> and <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/2017/08/22/stashing-is-latest-dating-trend-to-ruin-romances.html" target="_blank">stashing</a> were left behind in 2017, a new woe has bubbled up to vex sincere sentiments everywhere: mosting.</p>
              
                  <div class="ad-container tablet ad-h-250">
                      <div class="ad gpt" id="tablet-300x250"></div>
                  </div>
                  <div class="ad-container mobile ad-h-250">
                      <div class="ad gpt" id="mobile-320x50_300x250"></div>
                  </div>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p class="speakable">Comparable to <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/2017/08/23/this-man-ran-out-on-his-ex-in-most-brutal-way-and-now-shes-his-boss.html" target="_blank">ghosting</a>, the term was first coined by <a href="https://www.google.com/search?q=mel+magazine+mosting&amp;rlz=1C1GGRV_enUS757US757&amp;oq=mel+magazine+mosting&amp;aqs=chrome..69i57j69i60l3.2471j0j7&amp;sourceid=chrome&amp;ie=UTF-8" target="_blank">MEL Magazine</a> in January and has been trending on social media in recent days.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p>“Mosting is when someone goes overboard on the fluff job and then vanishes. It’s not just someone being complimentary and flattering; it’s someone faking being totally smitten when they aren’t,” journalist Tracy Moore wrote.&nbsp;“It’s the worst of the love crimes in many ways, because a lot of people can act charming just for sex, but only a certain breed of total phony will cry love.”</p>
              
             
                           		
					<div class="ad gpt ad-h-1" data-ad-pos="inread" data-ad-size="1x1"></div>
              
              
              
              


              
          
              <p><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/2018/03/19/curvy-instagrammers-inspiring-message-about-mr-6-pack-husband-goes-viral.html" target="_blank"><b>'CURVY' INSTAGRAMMER'S INSPIRING MESSAGE ABOUT 'MR. 6-PACK' HUSBAND GOES VIRAL</b></a><b></b></p>

              <p>Naturally, the Twitterverse has reacted to the dawn of “mosting” with heated emotion. Many slammed the “messed up” rise of false online flirtation as the crux of both this issue and other negative dating phenomenon.</p>
