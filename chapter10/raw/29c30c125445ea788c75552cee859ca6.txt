  <div class="article-show-container">
    <div class="article-body"><div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>Walmart has <a href="https://www.sciencealert.com/walmart-has-filed-a-patent-for-robot-bees-pollination-drones">just filed a patent</a> for autonomous, robot bees. Yes, that Walmart — and no, you didn’t slip into another, stranger dimension. The mega-corporation’s patent specifically covers “pollination drones.” These tiny robots could act just like bees, <a href="https://futurism.com/japan-has-created-black-mirror-inspired-bee-drones/">pollinating crops autonomously</a>.</p>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>The robot bees would operate using sensors and cameras to help them navigate to crops. Flying around autonomously, these drones could potentially pollinate as effectively as the real thing.</p>
</div>
<figure class="st__content-block st__content-block--image">
  <a href="https://wp-assets.futurism.com/2018/03/robot-bees.jpg"><img alt="  " src="https://assets.weforum.org/editor/kNwwWrRwA6o3potGqjmEbpWTgGkEfQLKJrUk5GXUPm0.jpg" /></a>
    <div class="image__caption">
      Robot bees could be the future of agriculture.
    </div>
    <div class="image__credit">
      Image: Polynoid/Greenpeace/Vimeo
    </div>
</figure>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>Oddly enough, this is not the only farming patent that Walmart has filed recently. <a href="https://www.cbinsights.com/research/walmart-farming-drones-patent/?mc_cid=b65788187a&amp;amp;mc_eid=aafe86284f">According to <i>CB Insights</i></a>, this is only one of six Walmart patents for farming drones that would do everything from monitor crop damage to spray pesticides. Incorporating autonomous robots into farming could cut costs and increase agriculture efficiency.</p>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>The thing that’s so puzzling about this move is: why Walmart?</p>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>The retailer hasn’t publicly commented on the patents yet, so the reasons behind Walmart’s sudden interest in farming drones has to be left up to interpretation. Yet since many Walmart locations do carry produce, it’s possible that the company is looking to gain more control of the food it’s selling. Perhaps by taking such a significant role in agriculture, the company will be able to improve quality and cut costs.</p>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--searchable-list list-group">
  <h3 class="list-group__header">
    <span class="list-group__title">Have you read?</span>
  </h3>
  <ul class="list-group__items">

      <li class="list-group__item">
        <a href="https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2015/09/is-humanity-just-a-phase-in-a-robotic-evolution">
          Is humanity just a phase in a robotic evolution?  
</a>      </li>
      <li class="list-group__item">
        <a href="https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2017/12/what-warring-wikipedia-bots-tell-us-about-our-robot-future">
          What warring Wikipedia bots tell us about our robot future  
</a>      </li>
  </ul>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>This seems like a sound explanation, especially since Walmart has <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/walmart-free-grocery-delivery-code-2018-3?IR=T">decided to expand its grocery delivery service</a>. According to a patent filed by the company in January, this service will allow shoppers to accept or reject produce. While such a service has potential to rake in serious business for Walmart, it also means they will need to step up the quality of their produce. Hence, potentially, the robo-bees.</p>
</div>
<div class="st__content-block st__content-block--text">
  <p>Meanwhile, <a href="https://futurism.com/strong-evidence-really-killing-bees/">honeybee populations are dying,</a> and we can no longer take pollination for granted. These small creatures are the backbone of agriculture and the food that we eat. While scientists work to better understand declining pollinator populations, and hopefully come up with solutions, these Walmart farming drones could keep agriculture, and fresh produce, alive.</p>
