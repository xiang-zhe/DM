              <div class="article_col-main article_col-main-left article_main">
                <div class="article-body">
                      <p> ROME — Pope Francis asked forgiveness Monday for all Christians who buy sex from women, saying men who frequent prostitutes are criminals with a "sick mentality" who think that women exist to be exploited. </p>
                      <p> "This isn't making love. This is torturing a woman. Let's not confuse the terms," Francis insisted. </p>
                    <figure class="img_half">
                      <noscript><img class="img-responsive img_inline" src="https://media3.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2018_12/2367951/180319-pope-francis-al-1200_87c30902ab08e700710bcf6f2683c2d5.nbcnews-fp-360-360.jpg" alt="Image: Pope Francis" title="Image: Pope Francis" /></noscript>
                      <a href="https://media3.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2018_12/2367951/180319-pope-francis-al-1200_87c30902ab08e700710bcf6f2683c2d5.nbcnews-ux-2880-1000.jpg" class="js-lightbox lightbox_link">
                        <button class="btn btn_overlay"><i class="fa fa-expand"></i></button>
                        <img class="img-responsive img_inline" src="https://media3.s-nbcnews.com/j/newscms/2018_12/2367951/180319-pope-francis-al-1200_87c30902ab08e700710bcf6f2683c2d5.nbcnews-ux-1024-900.jpg" alt="Image: Pope Francis" title="Image: Pope Francis"/>
                      </a>
                      <div class="js-caption_wrapper">
                        <figcaption class="img-caption img-caption_default no-margin-bottom"><span class="img-caption_txt">Pope Francis</span> <i class="fa fa-camera" aria-hidden="true"></i>Fabio Frustaci / EPA</figcaption>
                      </div>
                    </figure>
                  
                      <p> The pope made the comments during an intimate, four-hour-long listening session with 300 young people who were invited by the Vatican to Rome this week to help church leaders learn what kids these days think about the Catholic Church. </p>
                      <p> It's a preparatory meeting for a big synod of bishops in October on helping young people find their vocations in life. Francis has insisted that young adults — Catholic and not — be integral in the process informing the otherwise all-male, celibate and rather old church hierarchy about the future of the church. </p>
                    <div class="ad-container ad-container_default ad-container_mobile ad-hide ad-container-box1">
                    <div class="ad-label ad-hide">
                    	<a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/id/31066137/media-kit/" target="_blank">advertisement</a>
                    </div>
                    <div class="ad-content ad-xs box1" data-placement="box1"></div>
                    </div>
                      <p> "Young people must be taken seriously," he said. </p>
                      <p> He got an earful when he opened the meeting by urging the young people to speak with courage, without shame or "anesthesia" to dull the truth. </p>
                      <p> Nicholas Lopez, a college campus minister from Texas, told Francis that young people today face racism, poverty and gang violence, as well as "unjust immigration laws that threaten to split children from families." </p>
                      <p> Angela Markas from Australia told Francis young people want debate in the church about sexuality, same-sex attraction and the role of women. </p>
