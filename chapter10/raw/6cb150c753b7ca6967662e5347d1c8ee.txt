		<h1 class="headline head1">Wreck of the USS Juneau, famous for the deaths of the 5 Sullivan brothers, discovered in Pacific</h1>
		
		
		
		
			<div class="article-meta article-meta-lower">
				<div class="author-byline">

	                        </div>
	                   </div>
	                   <div class="info">
	                            <div class="caption">
		                            <h4 class="title"><a href="http://video.foxnews.com/v/5754981983001/sunken-wwii-ship-uss-juneau-discovered-by-paul-allen">Sunken WWII ship USS Juneau discovered by Paul Allen</a></h4>
		                                <p>Wreckage from the sunken WWII military ship USS Juneau was discovered on March 17, 2018 off the coast of the Solomon Islands by philanthropist and Microsoft co-founder Paul Allen&#x27;s expedition crew on the Research Vessel Petrel.</p>
	                        	</div>
					 </div>
				  </div>
              </div>
            
		 

    





								
								



 



       
       
      
          
              <p class="speakable">The wreck of the USS Juneau, which was sunk by a Japanese torpedo and lost 687 sailors in 1942, has been discovered by billionaire Paul Allen’s crew.</p>
              
                  <div class="ad-container tablet ad-h-250">
                      <div class="ad gpt" id="tablet-300x250"></div>
                  </div>
                  <div class="ad-container mobile ad-h-250">
                      <div class="ad gpt" id="mobile-320x50_300x250"></div>
                  </div>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p class="speakable">Five brothers from the Sullivan family were famously lost on the USS Juneau. Their story, which attracted widespread attention, was depicted in the 1944 movie “The Fighting Sullivans.” Two USS Navy ships have been named “The Sullivans” in memory of the brothers.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p>The brothers wanted to serve on the same ship, despite n<a href="https://www.history.navy.mil/browse-by-topic/disasters-and-phenomena/the-sullivan-brothers-and-the-assignment-of-family-members/sullivan-brothers-policy-family-members.html" target="_blank">aval policies</a> preventing siblings from serving together.</p>
              
             
                           		
					<div class="ad gpt ad-h-1" data-ad-pos="inread" data-ad-size="1x1"></div>
              
              
              
              


              
          
              <p><b><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/science/2018/03/05/uss-lexington-discovered-by-billionaire-paul-allens-crew-76-years-after-wwii-sinking.html" target="_blank">USS LEXINGTON DISCOVERED BY BILLIONAIRE PAUL ALLEN'S CREW 76 YEARS AFTER WWII SINKING</a></b></p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p>The USS Juneau was found on St. Patrick’s Day resting on the seafloor near the Solomon Islands. An autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV) from the research vessel Petrel first identified the wreck using sonar on March 17. The following day, a remotely operated underwater vehicle (ROV) was deployed from Petrel to verify the wreckage, capturing video footage of the Juneau.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              





	
    
    
    
	    <div class="inline  image-ct">
	    		
				<div class="m">
					 
		                
						
							<img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/images.foxnews.com/content/fox-news/science/2018/03/20/wreck-uss-juneau-famous-for-deaths-5-sullivan-brothers-discovered-in-pacific/_jcr_content/article-text/article-par-5/inline_spotlight_ima/image.img.jpg/612/344/1521578334772.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" alt="Juneau2">
						
					 
				</div>	
			
			
			 	        
		        	
		       	
			       	
			       		<div class="caption">
							<p>The prop of the USS Juneau resting on the seafloor. (Navigea, R/V Petrel)
								
					        </p>
				       	</div>
				    
		       	
	        
		</div>  
	



              
          
              <p>“We certainly didn’t plan to find the Juneau on St. Patrick’s Day. The variables of these searches are just too great,” said Robert Kraft, director of subsea operations for Paul Allen, in a <a href="https://www.paulallen.com/uss-juneau-wreck-located-famous-for-sullivan-brothers/#uss-juneau-wreck-located-famous-for-sullivan-brothers" target="_blank">statement</a>. “But finding the USS Juneau on Saint Patrick’s Day is an unexpected coincidence to the Sullivan brothers and all the service members who were lost 76 years ago.”</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
                  <div class="ad-container mobile ad-h-250">
                      <div class="ad gpt" id="mobile-300x251"></div>
                  </div>
              
          
              <p>Juneau was sunk on Nov. 13 1942 during the Battle of Guadalcanal. When a second torpedo hit her port side, an explosion cut the ship in half, killing most of the crew. The light cruiser sank in just 30 seconds. Around 115 of Juneau’s crew are believed to have survived the sinking, including, possibly, two of the Sullivan brothers. However, with U.S. forces concerned about the risk of further Japanese attacks, rescue efforts did not take place until eight days later. Only 10 men were rescued from the water.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p><b><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/science/2017/08/28/stunning-pictures-reveal-wreck-us-world-war-ii-era-sub.html" target="_blank">STUNNING PICTURES REVEAL THE WRECK OF US WORLD WAR II-ERA SUB</a></b></p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p>The first ship named after the brothers, USS The Sullivans (DD-537), was commissioned in 1943 and is now a museum ship in Buffalo. The second ship to bear the family name (DDG-68) is in active service as a guided missile destroyer.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              





	
    
    
    
	    <div class="inline  image-ct">
	    		
				<div class="m">
					 
		                
						
							<img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/images.foxnews.com/content/fox-news/science/2018/03/20/wreck-uss-juneau-famous-for-deaths-5-sullivan-brothers-discovered-in-pacific/_jcr_content/article-text/article-par-9/inline_spotlight_ima/image.img.jpg/612/344/1521579341873.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" alt="Sullivans">
						
					 
				</div>	
			
			
			 	        
		        	
		       	
			       	
			       		<div class="caption">
							<p>The Sullivan brothers photographed on board the USS Juneau, 14 Feb. 1942 From left to right: Joseph, Francis, Albert, Madison and George Sullivan (Courtesy U.S. Naval History and Heritage Command)
								
					        </p>
				       	</div>
				    
		       	
	        
		</div>  
	



              
          
              <p>“As the fifth commanding officer of USS The Sullivans (DDG 68), a ship named after five brothers, I am excited to hear that Allen and his team were able to locate the light cruiser USS Juneau (CL 52) that sunk during the Battle of Guadalcanal,” said Vice Adm. Rich Brown, commander, Naval Surface Forces, in a statement. “The story of the USS Juneau crew and Sullivan brothers epitomize the service and sacrifice of our nation’s greatest generation.”</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p>Expeditions led by Microsoft co-founder Paul Allen have discovered a host of historic military shipwrecks, such as the <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/science/2018/03/05/uss-lexington-discovered-by-billionaire-paul-allens-crew-76-years-after-wwii-sinking.html" target="_blank">USS Lexington</a>, which was located earlier this month, 76 years after it was lost in the Battle of the Coral Sea.</p>
              
             
               
              
              
              


              
          
              <p><b><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/science/2016/08/23/incredible-images-offer-first-glimpse-sunken-wwii-era-aircraft-carrier.html">INCREDIBLE IMAGES OFFER FIRST GLIMPSE OF SUNKEN WWII-ERA AIRCRAFT CARRIER</a></b></p>
              
             
               
              
              
              





	
    
    
    
	    <div class="inline  image-ct">
	    		
				<div class="m">
					 
		                
						
							<img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/images.foxnews.com/content/fox-news/science/2018/03/20/wreck-uss-juneau-famous-for-deaths-5-sullivan-brothers-discovered-in-pacific/_jcr_content/article-text/article-par-12/inline_spotlight_ima/image.img.jpg/612/344/1521579275491.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" alt="Juneau">
						
					 
				</div>	
			
			
			 	        
		        	
		       	
			       	
			       		<div class="caption">
							<p>The USS Juneau In New York Harbor, 11 Feb. 1942. (Courtesy the U.S. National Archives)
								
					        </p>
				       	</div>
				    
		       	
	        
		</div>  
	



              
          
              <p>Last year Allen’s crew found the long-lost wreck of the <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/us/2017/08/19/long-lost-uss-indianapolis-wreckage-discovered-by-paul-allen-crew.html" target="_blank">USS Indianapolis</a> in the Philippine Sea. The sinking of the Indianapolis, which delivered the Hiroshima bomb and is mentioned in the movie &quot;Jaws,&quot; in 1945 resulted in the greatest single loss of life at sea in the U.S. Navy’s history. Of 1,196 crew aboard the heavy cruiser, only 317 survived.</p>
