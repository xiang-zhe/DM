      <img src="/contentassets/20c4948e2d05495395c1f06886056881/5ab294acb028471f008b46b8.png?preset=article-image" width="1010" height="505" />
      <dl>
        <dt>caption</dt>
        <dd id="image-caption-5ab294acb028471f008b46b8">Donald Trump Jr.</dd>
        <dt id="image-source">source</dt>

      <p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <strong>A former Indian official accused Donald Trump Jr. of
    pushing an illegal project in Mumbai.</strong>
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>The accusation comes as Trump Jr. has received scrutiny
    for a recent India trip.</strong>
        </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
  A former Indian official accused Donald Trump Jr. of pushing a
  "blatantly illegal" project in Mumbai.
</p> <div style="margin-top:20px;"><div class="bam-ad-slot" data-slot-name="articlemodule"></div><div class="bam-ad-slot" data-slot-name="mob"></div><p style="display:none">Ad</p></div>
      <p>
  The official, Chief Minister Prithviraj Chavan, whose position is
  the equivalent of a US governor, said on the <a href="https://www.propublica.org/article/trump-inc-podcast-donald-trump-jr-india-project">latest edition of ProPublica's
  "Trump Inc." podcast</a> that Trump Jr. had asked him to overturn
  a decision to revoke permits for the Trump Organization's initial
  India project.
</p>
      <p>
  That project was halted after investigators found
  "irregularities," ProPublica wrote, adding that those probing the
  project were tipped off by a state lawmaker who suspect a $100
  million fraud and "gross violations."
</p>
      <p>
  Trump Jr. then traveled to India in April 2012 along with
  partners and met with Chavan in hopes of getting the project up
  and running again.
</p>
      <p>
  Chavan was told that Trump Jr. and the partners wanted to discuss
  investing in his state. Instead, they asked him to overturn that
  earlier decision.
</p>
      <p>
  "I would get into trouble to sanction something that was
  blatantly illegal," he said, adding the plans were "not within
  the existing rules."
</p>
      <p>
  The Trump Organization did break into the Indian market in 2014
  under a new government with the construction of Trump Tower
  Mumbai. That project is set to be finished in 2019 and is one of
  five Trump-affiliated projects being developed in the country.
</p>
      <p>
  Just last month, Trump Jr. visited India to promote the
  properties. His visit <a href="http://money.cnn.com/2018/02/20/news/economy/donald-trump-jr-india-visit-delhi-kolkata-modi/index.html">came under fire</a> by critics,
  particularly after national newspaper ads promised a dinner with
  President Donald Trump's eldest son in exchange for an early
  sign-up to one of the Trump apartments under construction.
</p>
      <p>
  ProPublica reported that a Trump partner said they booked $15
  million in sales within one day during Trump Jr.'s India visit.
</p>
      <p>
  Each of the Trump Organization's India projects are licensing
  deals in which the company provides the Trump name and manages
  the property.
</p>
      <p>
  The Trump Organization didn't respond to requests for comment.
</p>
