        ‘We can suddenly come’: Turkey’s Erdogan puts all Kurdish-held towns in Syria & Iraq on notice

    </h1><div class="article__date"><time class="date date_article-header">Published time: 20 Mar, 2018 04:40 </time><time class="date date_article-header">Edited time: 20 Mar, 2018 07:18 </time></div><div class="article__short-url"><div class="short-url"><a class="short-url__link" href="https://on.rt.com/91fm">Get short URL</a></div></div><div class="article__cover"><div class="media  "><img class="media__item " src="https://cdni.rt.com/files/2018.03/article/5aafd28ffc7e9391608b4601.jpg" alt="‘We can suddenly come’: Turkey’s Erdogan puts all Kurdish-held towns in Syria &amp; Iraq on notice"></div><div class="media__footer media__footer_bottom "><div class="media__title media__title_arcticle">

        Turkey’s military operation in Syria will target other Kurdish-held towns – and may even spill over into Iraq – President Recep Tayyip Erdogan has announced just a day after pro-Turkish forces seized Afrin.



    </div><div class="article__text text "><p>The controversial cross-border offensive <em>&ldquo;will go on until the terror corridor through Manbij, Ayn al-Arab, Tell Abyad, Ras al-Ayn, Qamishli has been wiped out,&rdquo;</em> Erdogan <a href="https://aa.com.tr/en/middle-east/erdogan-says-turkey-to-continue-military-operation/1093414" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">said</a>, speaking in the presidential complex in Ankara on Monday.</p><p>Erdogan hinted that the Turkish military operation may even expand into neighboring Iraq, if needed, in an effort to <em>&ldquo;eliminate&rdquo;</em> forces loyal to the Kurdish Workers&rsquo; Party (PKK), which Ankara has designated as a terrorist organization.</p><div class="arcticle__read-more read-more">
    <div class="read-more__title">Read more</div>

    </a>
</div><p><em>&ldquo;We can suddenly come over one night in Iraq's Sinjar and eliminate PKK terrorists there,&rdquo;</em> Erdogan said, according to Turkish state media.</p><p>On January 20, Turkey launched a cross-border offensive into Syria with an aim to dislodge Kurdish <em>&ldquo;terrorists&rdquo;</em> from Afrin. The assault, codenamed Operation Olive Branch, has strained relations between Ankara and Washington. The Kurdish YPG are key US allies in the fight against Islamic State (IS, formerly ISIS/ISIL) terrorists, but Ankara views them as an offshoot of the terrorist-designated Kurdistan Workers&rsquo; Party (PKK).</p><p>The Turkish leader&rsquo;s announcement comes just one day after the Turkish military, aided by its Free Syrian Army (FSA) allies, seized the Syrian city of Afrin. Erdogan said that capturing Afrin was a <em>&ldquo;comma&rdquo;</em> and <em>&ldquo;God willing, a full stop will come next.&rdquo;</em> However, he stressed that Turkey was not invading Syria.</p><p><em>&ldquo;Our intention is not to invade, but to carry out operations to cleanse terrorists and eliminate terrorist threats to our country,&rdquo;</em> he said, as <a href="http://www.rudaw.net/english/middleeast/19032018" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">cited</a> by Rudaw.</p><p>Thousands have reportedly fled their homes in the Afrin region in the wake of Turkey&rsquo;s offensive. After announcing the city&rsquo;s capture, Erdogan vowed to protect Afrin&rsquo;s residents. However, Kurdish leaders have accused the Turkish military and its proxies of committing<em> &ldquo;massacres&rdquo;</em> and<em> &ldquo;ethnic cleansing.&rdquo;</em></p><p><strong><em>Think your friends would be interested?
