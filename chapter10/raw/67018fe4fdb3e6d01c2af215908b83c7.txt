<section class="article__headline-section">
			<span class="article__kicker theme__copy-color">VOTE SCANDAL</span>
		<h1 class="article__headline">Damning Russian ‘election rigging’ pictures show voters casting Putin ballots at more than one polling station</h1></section>

		<div class="article__subdeck theme__border-color">
		<p>The claims come after Putin comfortably extended his rule of Russia for another six years  winning 76.67 per cent of the vote - his highest score ever</p>
	</div>
	</header><!-- .article__header -->

<div class="article__meta">

	
	<div class="article__name-date">
					<div class="article__author">
				<span class="article__author-name">
					By Jon Lockett				</span>
			</div>
		
		<div class="article__time">
			<div class="article__published">
				<span>21st March 2018,</span>
				<span class="article__timestamp">3:55 pm</span>
			</div>

					<div class="article__updated">
				<span>Updated: 21st March 2018,</span>
				<span class="article__timestamp">5:59 pm</span>
			</div>
				</div>

	</div>
</div><!-- .article__meta -->
								<div class="article__content">
									<p class="article__content article__content--intro">PHOTOS seem show Russian state workers &#8220;voting multiple times&#8221; in the country&#8217;s presidential election.</p>
<p>The images come after Vladimir Putin&#8217;s record victory has already been soured by claims the ballots were rigged in his favour.</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="0">

		<a href="#"><p><img class="wp-image-2339949 size-thesun-article-image" alt="This woman &#8211; a state health official &#8211; was spotted voting three times" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/nintchdbpict000393751721.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="406" /></p></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Reuters		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		This woman &#8211; a state health official &#8211; was spotted voting three times	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>Many of those caught on camera turned up to the polling stations in groups and in state-run minibuses.</p>
<p>One woman caught voting at least THREE TIMES was identified as a hospital deputy director of health and safety.</p>
<p>Voting twice is a misdemeanour under Russian law and those caught are heavily fined.</p>
<p>But when shown these pictures, election commission member Leila Koichuyeva said: &#8220;They could be twins.&#8221;</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="1">

		<a href="#"><p><img class="wp-image-2339950 size-thesun-article-image" alt="Journalists uncovered a loophole which allows people to vote in more than one location" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/nintchdbpict000393751708.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="611" /></p></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Reuters		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Journalists uncovered a loophole which allows people to vote in more than one location	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>Respected news agency Reuters was able to speak to seven of 17 people photographed casting multiple votes.</p>
<p>They either denied voting more than once or declined to comment.</p>
<p>Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov said they would only take the images seriously if there was further evidence.</p>
<p>He said: &#8220;If they (the photos) are not backed up, then it does not worry us at all.&#8221;</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="2">

		<a href="#"><p><img class="wp-image-2339951 size-thesun-article-image" alt="This man in a hat was spotted casting his vote at separate polling stations" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/nintchdbpict000393751693.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="602" /></p></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Reuters		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		This man in a hat was spotted casting his vote at separate polling stations	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>Reporters monitored 12 polling stations around the country and witnessed other irregularities.</p>
<p>In all 12 polling stations, the turnout declared by election officials exceeded a tally kept by Reuters of how many people voted.</p>
<p>In one case in Simferopol the difference between the two figures was significant: 528 votes, or 66 percent of the votes cast.</p>
<p>The journalists also uncovered a loophole in the registration system which allows people to vote in more than one location.</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="3">

		<a href="#"><p><img class="wp-image-2339952 size-thesun-article-image" alt="Voting twice is a misdemeanour under Russian law and those caught are heavily fined" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/nintchdbpict000393751697.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="610" /></p></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Reuters		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Voting twice is a misdemeanour under Russian law and those caught are heavily fined	</figcaption>
	</figure>

	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="4">

		<a href="#"><p><img class="wp-image-2339953 size-thesun-article-image" alt="Reporters monitored 12 polling stations around the vast country" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/nintchdbpict000393751704.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="608" /></p></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Reuters		</div>

		</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Reporters monitored 12 polling stations around the vast country	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>Another woman also appeared to vote twice on Sunday.</p>
<p>When approached the woman said her name was Jamila Tebueva who worked for the local town hall.</p>
<p>She said she voted only once, and went to a second polling station to accompany friends.</p>
<p>When told she had been photographed with a voting slip in her hand at the second location she said: &#8220;Is it alright if I don&#8217;t reply?&#8221;</p>
