<p>Britain's post-<a href="/topic/brexit">Brexit</a> blue passports are set to be produced by a firm in mainland Europe, according to reports.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>Swapping the EU’s burgundy passport for the old blue version has been hailed by Brexiteers as a symbol of Britain’s regained independence.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>But Franco-Dutch firm Gemalto has won the contract to manufacture the document, the Telegraph reported.</p>

    

              
                
        
<div id="mpu0ArticleBody" class="ad-wrapper">
    
</div>

    

              
                
        
<div id="teads" class="ad-wrapper">
    
</div>

    

              
                
        
<p>The firm undercut rival bids by around £50 million, the newspaper said.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>Tory MP Sir Bill Cash, chairman of the Commons European Scrutiny Committee, branded such a move "completely wrong and unnecessary".</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>The tender to produce the passport was understood to have been put out across the EU under single market rules.</p>

</div>

<p>Sir Bill said: "I think it is incongruous to say the least. It is completely unnecessary and it is symbolically completely wrong.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"Whatever the conditions which led to the decision in terms of pricing, the fact is that this is a symbolic event."</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>Former cabinet minister Priti Patel told The Sun: "This should be a moment that we should be celebrating. The return of our iconic blue passport will re-establish the British identity.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"But to be putting the job in the hands of the French is simply astonishing. It is a national humiliation.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"I would urge Amber Rudd and the Government to look again at the powers they have to see what they can do."</p>

<p>Liberal Democrat Brexit spokesman Tom Brake said: "The blue passport saga is turning into a farce.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"First it was established that we did not have to leave the EU to have blue passports.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"Now we learn that the passports will be printed by a foreign company. And to add insult to injury, we will pay over the odds for them because the value of the pound has fallen since Brexit and they will have to be imported.</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>"Brexit is far too high a price to pay for a blue passport."</p>

    

              
                
        
<p>The head of pro-Europe campaign group Best For Britain, Eloise Todd, said: "The new the pro-Brexit blue passports were supposed to be a statement of intent and now we find out they are to be made by the French or the Dutch. The irony is unreal.</p>
