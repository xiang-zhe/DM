Nader and Elliot Broidy, a longtime GOP fundraiser, used their influence and contacts in Trump's White House to &quot;cultivate&quot; Trump on behalf of the UAE and Saudi Crown Prince (and self-proclaimed Jared Kushner puppet-master) Mohammed bin Salman, and against Iran and Qatar, the Times says, adding: &quot;Tillerson was fired last week, and the president has adopted tough approaches toward both Iran and Qatar.&quot; The two men — Nader, 58, and Broidy, 60 — met during Trump's inaugural festivities and &quot;became fast friends,&quot; and Nader didn't come to the friendship empty-handed, the Times explains:
Nader tempted ... Broidy with the prospect of more than $1 billion in contracts for his private security company, Circinus, and he helped deliver deals worth more than $200 million with the United Arab Emirates. He also flattered Mr. Broidy about &quot;how well you handle Chairman,&quot; a reference to Mr. Trump, and repeated to his well-connected friend that he told the effective rulers of both Saudi Arabia and the UAE about &quot;the Pivotal Indispensable Magical Role you are playing to help them.&quot; [The New York Times]

In return, Broidy told Nader he personally pushed Trump in October to fire Tillerson, seen by the Saudis and Emiratis as insufficiently hardline on Iran and Qatar, and urged Trump to meet with the UAE crown prince in a &quot;quiet&quot; place outside the White House — a request blocked by National Security Adviser H.R. McMaster, Broidy reported. Nader was met by Mueller's agents in February en route to meet Trump at Mar-a-Lago, an invitation wrangled by Broidy. You can read more about the tangled web at The New York Times. Peter Weber"/>

												<div class="sp-text black-rollover underline" id="sp-body-762343">
							 <p>George Nader, a political adviser to the crown prince leading the United Arab Emirates and a <a href="http://theweek.com/speedreads/759453/report-adviser-emirates-ties-trump-aides-cooperating-mueller" target="_blank">cooperating witness in Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation</a>, has spent the past year working with the Republican National Committee's deputy finance chairman to steer President Trump's Middle East policy and oust Secretary of State Rex Tillerson, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/21/us/politics/george-nader-elliott-broidy-uae-saudi-arabia-white-house-influence.html" target="_blank"><em>The New York Times</em> reports</a>, citing interviews and newly disclosed documents.</p>
<p>Nader and Elliot Broidy, a longtime GOP fundraiser, used their influence and contacts in Trump's White House to "cultivate" Trump on behalf of the UAE and Saudi Crown Prince (and <a href="http://theweek.com/speedreads/762334/saudi-crown-prince-reportedly-bragged-kushner-pocket" target="_blank">self-proclaimed Jared Kushner puppet-master</a>) Mohammed bin Salman, and against Iran and Qatar, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/21/us/politics/george-nader-elliott-broidy-uae-saudi-arabia-white-house-influence.html" target="_blank">the <em>Times</em> says,</a> adding: "Tillerson was <a href="http://theweek.com/speedreads/760605/rex-tillerson-reportedly-found-fired-twitter" target="_blank">fired last week</a>, and the president has adopted tough approaches toward both Iran and Qatar." The two men — Nader, 58, and Broidy, 60 — met during Trump's inaugural festivities and "became fast friends," and Nader didn't come to the friendship empty-handed, the <em>Times</em> explains:</p>
<blockquote><p>Nader tempted ... Broidy with the prospect of more than $1 billion in contracts for his private security company, Circinus, and he helped deliver deals worth more than $200 million with the United Arab Emirates. He also flattered Mr. Broidy about "how well you handle Chairman," a reference to Mr. Trump, and repeated to his well-connected friend that he told the effective rulers of both Saudi Arabia and the UAE about "the Pivotal Indispensable Magical Role you are playing to help them." [<em><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/21/us/politics/george-nader-elliott-broidy-uae-saudi-arabia-white-house-influence.html" target="_blank">The New York Times</a></em>]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In return, Broidy told Nader he personally pushed Trump in October to fire Tillerson, seen by the Saudis and Emiratis as insufficiently hardline on Iran and Qatar, and urged Trump to meet with the UAE crown prince in a "quiet" place outside the White House — a request blocked by National Security Adviser H.R. McMaster, Broidy reported. Nader was <a href="http://theweek.com/speedreads/761105/key-figure-trumprussia-investigation-also-convicted-pedophile" target="_blank">met by Mueller's agents</a> in February en route to meet Trump at Mar-a-Lago, an invitation wrangled by Broidy. You can <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/21/us/politics/george-nader-elliott-broidy-uae-saudi-arabia-white-house-influence.html" target="_blank">read more about the tangled web at <em>The New York Times</em></a>. <a class="author" href=/authors/peter-weber><em>Peter Weber</em></a></p>

But a buzzkilling <a href="https://genome.cshlp.org/content/early/2018/03/21/gr.223693.117" target="_blank">new study</a> published Thursday found that the bones simply belonged to a human with a series of bone mutations.</p>
<p>The 6-inch skeleton — dubbed Ata by researchers and alien aficionados — displays an unusual series of DNA mutations, reports <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/22/science/ata-mummy-alien-chile.html" target="_blank"><em>The New York </em></a><em><a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/22/science/ata-mummy-alien-chile.html" target="_blank">Times</a></em>. <p>That explains the utterly bizarre appearance of Ata, whose tiny frame has just ten ribs, rather than 12; a pointy, elongated skull; and large, alien-like eye sockets. <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr" xml:lang="en">There have been many sad moments for Toys"R"Us in recent weeks, and none more heartbreaking than today's news about the passing of our beloved founder, Charles Lazarus.
