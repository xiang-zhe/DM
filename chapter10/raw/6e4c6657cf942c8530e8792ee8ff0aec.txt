<h2>Police Scotland reaction</h2>
<p>Detective Chief Superintendent Gerry Mclean, of Police Scotland's Organised Crime and Counter Terrorism Unit, said: "Following Ward's arrest a vast amount of material was seized by officers who invested a significant amount of time into quantifying the scale of Ward's interest in terrorism. </p><p>"Although he operated alone, the weapons, manuals and downloads he possessed had the potential to cause serious harm.</p><p>"While we may never know the full extent of Ward's intentions thanks to the early intervention of police, we do know that we cannot underestimate the dangerous nature of any behaviour or activity linked to terrorism.</p><p>"These acts are very rare, not least in the North East of Scotland, and while concerted action takes place every day alongside our partners to protect the public it is crucial that communities remain vigilant."</p>
