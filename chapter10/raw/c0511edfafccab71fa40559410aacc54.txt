<p>More than 20 kids have been hospitalised after a possible hydrogen sulphide leak at the nearby Yadrovo dump, Russia&#x27;s TASS news agency reported.</p>
<figure class="article__media"><div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="0"><a href="#"><img alt=" Locals in Volokolamsk, near Moscow, are believed to have been poisoned by a chemical leak from the nearby Yadrovo landfill" class="lazyload wp-image-5864986 size-thesun-article-image" data-src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/rb-map-russia-v2new1.jpg?strip=all&w=750" height="500" width="750"/></a><div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80"><a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color"></span></a></div></div><figcaption class="article__media-caption"> Locals in Volokolamsk, near Moscow, are believed to have been poisoned by a chemical leak from the nearby Yadrovo landfill</figcaption></figure>
<p>Footage from Volokolamsk, home to just 23,000 people, shows hundreds of people queuing for treatment outside the local hospital.</p>
<p>They were all reported to be suffering from the same symptoms - dizziness and nausea.</p>
<p>A local source told <a href="http://tass.ru/proisshestviya/5051658?utm_source=twitter.com&amp;utm_medium=social&amp;utm_campaign=smm_social_share" target="_blank" rel="noopener">TASS</a>: &quot;The teachers noticed the children began to feel unwell and immediately called the hospital.&quot;</p>
<p>Local education chief Ekaterina Volkova later revealed the symptoms were a result of a suspected hydrogen sulphide leak.</p>
<figure class="article__media"><div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="1"><a href="#"><img alt=" Footage from Volokolamsk, Moscow, showed hundreds queuing outside a hospital after a suspected chemical leak" class="lazyload size-thesun-article-image wp-image-5864077" data-src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/russia11.png?strip=all&w=960" height="697" width="1272"/></a><div class="article__credit">Denis Kunaev</div><div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80"><a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color"></span></a></div></div><figcaption class="article__media-caption"> Footage from Volokolamsk, Moscow, showed hundreds queuing outside a hospital after a suspected chemical leak</figcaption></figure>
<p>Earlier this month, riot police were called in to disperse protesters from Volokolamsk who complained their town was being polluted by noxious gases.</p>
<p>Demonstrators blocked a road leading to the Yadrovo landfill on March 8 and prevented rubbish trucks from entering the town.</p>
<p>Volokolamsk police said around nine protesters were briefly arrested before they were released without charge, <a href="https://www.rferl.org/a/russia-moscow-protesters-violently-dispersed-yadrovo-garbage-dump/29089027.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Radio Free Europe reported</a>.</p>
