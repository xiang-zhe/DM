Analytica (CA), the British firm at the centre of the Facebook data storm.</p><p>CA is at the centre of an international scandal after The Observer revealed it harvested the data of 50 million US voters on Facebook. A Channel 4 News investigation also caught the company's bosses boasting about using honeytraps, bribes, and fake news in order to influence elections.</p>In Prime Minister's Questions today, the SNP's Westminster leader, Ian Blackford, read out a list of alleged links between Cambridge Analytica and the prime minister's Conservative Party.<div align="center" style="width:300px;margin:auto;" data-ad-slot="btf300bellyp_6_63400037" data-fn="adrhs4_belly('[data-ad-slot=&quot;btf300bellyp_6_63400037&quot;]','web')"></div><p>"Can I point out that the parent company of Cambridge Analytica is Strategic Communications Laboratories. It has been run by a chairman of Oxford Conservative Association," Blackford said.</p><p>This was a reference to Julian Wheatland, who is pictured below on the campaign trail with former Prime Minister David Cameron. Wheatland was chairman of CA's parent company SCL Group.</p><p>"Its founding chairman was a former Conservative MP. A director has donated over &pound;700,000 to the Tory party," Blackford continued.</p><p>"A former Conservative party treasurer is a shareholder.</p><p>"We know about the links to the Conservative party - they go on and on. Will the prime minister confirm to the House, her government's connections to the company?"</p><h2>Watch Blackford quiz May on Cambridge Analytica links</h2>The prime minister refused to address the specific cases mentioned but described allegations against Cambridge Analytica as "very concerning" and called for the company and others to comply fully with investigations.<div align="center" style="width:300px;margin:auto;" data-ad-slot="btf300bellyp_13_63400037" data-fn="adrhs4_belly('[data-ad-slot=&quot;btf300bellyp_13_63400037&quot;]','web')"></div><p>"The right honourable gentleman has been talking about two companies. The parent company, SCL, and Cambridge Analytica. I can say to him that as far as I'm aware, the government has no current contracts with Cambridge Analytica or with the SCL group," May said.</p><p>"The allegations against Cambridge Analytica are clearly very concerning and it is absolutely right that they should be properly investigated.</p><p>"It's right that the Information Commissioner is doing exactly that because people need to have confidence in how their personal data is being used.</p><p>"I would expect Facebook, Cambridge Analytica, and all organisations involved to comply fully with the investigation taking place."</p><div><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en"><p>Out campaigning this morning with <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/David_Cameron?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw" rel="nofollow">@David_Cameron</a> <a target="_blank" href="http://t.co/vTsCOQ2gwa" rel="nofollow">pic.twitter.com/vTsCOQ2gwa</a></p>- Julian Wheatland  (@JulianWheatland) <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/mims/statuses/584348108403904512?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw" rel="nofollow">April 4, 2015</a></blockquote></div><div><blockquote class="twitter-video" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Theresa May asked about Conservative links to Cambridge Analytica and its parent company <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/PMQs?src=hash&amp;ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw" rel="nofollow">#PMQs</a>  <a target="_blank" href="https://t.co/SVE6idtA6U" rel="nofollow">https://t.co/SVE6idtA6U</a> <a target="_blank" href="https://t.co/d4C9Ni9YoV" rel="nofollow">pic.twitter.com/d4C9Ni9YoV</a></p>- Wanda Rees (@WandaReesMs) <a target="_blank" href="https://twitter.com/WandaReesMs/status/976437592332922881?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw" rel="nofollow">March 21, 2018</a></blockquote></div><div><blockquote class="twitter-video" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">"People need to have confidence in how their personal data is being used" -
