  <p>Police in Arizona have released a short video of a <a href="/news/2018-03-20/uber-suspends-self-driving-car-tests-after-fatal-crash/9565586" target="_self" title="">fatal collision between an Uber self-driving vehicle and a pedestrian</a>, as investigators probe the accident that has put new focus on the safety of autonomous vehicles.</p><p>The video, taken from inside the Volvo XC90 sport utility vehicle that Uber has used for testing, shows the vehicle driving along a dark road when a woman walking a bicycle across the road appears in the headlights.</p><p>The woman, Elaine Herzberg, 49, later died from her injuries.</p><p>Police have released few details about the accident that occurred on Sunday night in Tempe, Arizona, a suburb of Phoenix, while the SUV was driving in autonomous mode.</p><div class="inline-content wysiwyg right">
<div class="inner">
  <h2><a href="/news/2017-03-11/everything-you-need-to-know-about-driverless-cars/8336322" target="_self" title="">The driverless car revolution</a></h2><a href="/news/2017-03-11/everything-you-need-to-know-about-driverless-cars/8336322" target="_self" title=""><img alt="" class="float--none" src="/cm/lb/8389280/data/uber-self-driving-car-data.jpg"/> </a> <br/><a href="/news/2017-03-11/everything-you-need-to-know-about-driverless-cars/8336322" target="_self" title="">Soon, you'll be able to do all the things you'd rather be doing while driving. </a></div>
</div><p> </p><p>Uber suspended its self-driving testing in North America after the incident and federal safety regulators are conducting their own probe.</p><p>A photo released by safety regulators on Tuesday showed that the impact occurred on the right side of the vehicle.</p><p>The video also shows the driver at the wheel, who appears to be looking down throughout most of the video.</p><p>Just before the video stops, the driver looks up and appears shocked.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"The video is disturbing and heartbreaking to watch, and our thoughts continue to be with Elaine's loved ones," Uber said in a statement.</p></blockquote><p>"Our cars remain grounded, and we're assisting local, state and federal authorities in any way we can."</p><div class="inline-content video full">
