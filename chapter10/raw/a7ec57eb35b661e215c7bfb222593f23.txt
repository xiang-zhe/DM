During this time there will also be a public hearing on the tariffs. </p><p> After the comment period, the USTR will determine which goods the tariffs will apply to and release those findings. </p><p> All in all, it could be well into May or June before the tariffs are fully implemented. </p><h2> Trade-war fears sink stocks </h2><p> The new tariffs mark an escalation in the ongoing trade spat between the US and China.

For businesses that use the imports in their end products, the tariffs will most likely drive up costs and lead to increased prices or cuts in other areas of business. </p><p> In the event of a trade war, the economic consequence would be much more severe and could even <a href=http://www.businessinsider.com/trump-steel-tariff-china-germany-japan-global-recession-2017-6 > drive the US into a recession</a>. There could be serious consequences for growth even if the fight stays between the US and China, according to Shawn Golhar, the head of public-policy research at Barclays. </p><p> "In terms of China, the US trade deficit is $375.2 billion, so double-digit, broad-based tariffs could have a deleterious effect on economic growth," Golhar said in a note to clients. "Shortly after the 2016 election, our economists estimated that a 15% tariff on Chinese imports would trim growth 0.3-0.5 percentage points over a four-quarter horizon, assuming China imposes similar tariffs on US exports." </p><p> Given the possibility of a sustained trade fight, investors were jittery on Thursday. The Dow Jones industrial average <a href=http://www.businessinsider.com/stock-market-today-selloff-trump-china-tariff-escalates-trade-war-fears-2018-3 > sank more than 400 points</a> leading up to Trump's announcement. </p></div>
