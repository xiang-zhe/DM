<h1 itemprop="headline name">Macron: EU &#8216;mad&#8217; to do trade deal with US after Paris climate withdrawal </h1>
<time class="news-date">Published on <span itemprop="datePublished" content="2018-03-22T16:44:41+00:00">22/03/2018, 4:44pm</span></time>
<meta itemprop="dateModified" content="2018-03-22T17:09:05+00:00" />
<p class="news-excerpt" itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">&#8216;I&#8217;m not in favour of showing any softness to those who decide to break those rules,&#8217; the French president said about the deal struck in his capital in 2015</p>
<div class="wp-caption" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/ImageObject">
<img width="800" height="450" src="http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb.jpg" class="img-responsive wp-post-image" alt="" itemprop="image" srcset="http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb.jpg 800w, http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb-112x63.jpg 112w, http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb-300x168.jpg 300w, http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb-768x432.jpg 768w, http://www.climatechangenews.com/files/2018/03/b7594d9d-1521-403e-8da3-57d0d6e5ecfb-600x337.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 800px) 100vw, 800px" /> <p class="wp-caption-text" itemprop="caption">Donald Trump and Emmanuel Macron (Left photo:Gage Skidmore/Flickr, Right photo:LeWeb/Wikimedia)</p>
</div>
<p class="news-author">By
<span itemprop="author">
<a href="www.climatechangenews.com/author/arthur-neslen/">Arthur Neslen</a> in Brussels </span>
</p>
<div class="news-content" itemprop="articleBody"><p class="p1"><em><span class="s1">Climate Home News is one of the world’s most trusted sources of climate politics news. <a href="http://www.climatechangenews.com/newsletter-sign-up/">Sign up</a> for our newsletter.</span></em></p>
<p class="p1"><strong><span class="s1">French president Emmanuel Macron has warned the EU would be “mad” to sign a trade agreement with countries that refused to honour the Paris climate agreement.</span><span class="s2"><br />
</span></strong></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Macron was speaking a day after the Irish Taoiseach, Leo Varadkar, <a href="https://www.independent.ie/irish-news/politics/taoiseachs-pitch-of-new-euus-trade-deal-well-received-by-president-trump-36711233.html"><span class="s4">implored Trump to consider</span></a> “a new trade deal between the US and the EU”, as a way of lowering trade tensions over steel and aluminium tariffs.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Speaking at a conference in Brussels, Macron said that since Trump had indicated US withdrawal from the Paris deal, a new trade deal would undermine clean energy producers in Europe, and project a message of weakness abroad.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Far from bending to the will of the US, “trade agreements should reverse the burden of proof,” Macron said. “They should be a way of spreading our standards. Anyone who signs an agreement with the EU should be committing to put the Paris Agreement into practice&#8230;</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">“We even go beyond our own rules and ask our own economic agents to do the same. Why should we sign a trade agreement with powers that say they don&#8217;t want to implement the Paris Agreement? We would be mad [to do so].”</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Macron said that the world&#8217;s multilateral rules-based trade policy was “threatened by unilateral temptations”.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">“I&#8217;m not in favour of showing any softness to those who decide to break those rules,” he said. “We can no longer pursue objectives that work against our own [climate] policies inside our own borders.&#8221;</span></p>
<blockquote>
<p class="p2"><a href="http://www.climatechangenews.com/2018/02/02/eu-difficult-imagine-trade-deals-countries-not-paris-agreement/"><strong>EU:</strong> &#8216;No new trade deals with countries not in Paris Agreement&#8217;</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Doing so would “discourage” Europe&#8217;s clean energy investors who might suffer from unfair competition as a result, he added.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">The French leader also made an impassioned plea for a European carbon floor price, and a border tax to prevent cheap, carbon-intensive products entering the EU market which could undermine cleaner local producers.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Macron called for the EU&#8217;s budget &#8211; currently being negotiated &#8211; to increase the fifth of budget spending currently earmarked for climate measures. This is likely to upset some other EU states, such as Poland, which <a href="http://www.climatechangenews.com/2018/02/02/poland-put-common-sense-climate-ambition-host-critical-un-talks/">has painted climate action</a> as a drag on economic growth.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">“In the [European] Council debate, we have to set an objective that none of the expenditure is hostile to the climate,” he said. “Setting climate spending at 40% of the budget would be ambitious compared to today but I think we can achieve it.”</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Macron&#8217;s oration brought what would normally have been a staid sustainable finance conference to its feet for a one minute standing ovation, with attendees enthralled at the sight of an EU leader &#8216;walking the walk&#8217; in the face of Trumpian assault on Paris.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">“I think he&#8217;s right,” Christiana Figueres, the former UN climate chief told Climate Home News immediately after hearing Macron&#8217;s comments linking trade deals to the Paris accord. “What more can I say?”</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Last month, CHN reported the French foreign minister, <a href="http://www.climatechangenews.com/2018/02/02/france-us-no-paris-agreement-no-trade-agreement-1/"><span class="s5">Jean-Baptiste Lemoyne,</span><span class="s4"> said</span></a> that if Trump withdrew the US form the Paris agreement, there would be “no trade agreement &#8211; the US knows what to expect”.</span></p>
<p class="p3"><span class="s6">That position was subsequently <span class="s4"><a href="http://www.climatechangenews.com/2018/02/02/eu-difficult-imagine-trade-deals-countries-not-paris-agreement/">backed up by the European Commission</a> and the practice is already implemented in some of the EU&#8217;s latest trade deals</span>. Macron&#8217;s comments on Thursday were the first time a European head of state has referred to the precondition.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Earlier in the day, the UN&#8217;s special climate envoy, Michael Bloomberg, told the conference that under Trump, Washington was “out of synch with the national zeitgeist but it matters less”.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">US cities and regions representing emissions roughly equivalent to the UK, France and Germany combined remained committed to the Paris Agreement, he said.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">In a bid partly intended to reverse the shortfall in climate funding caused by the US&#8217; reneging on Obama-era spending commitments [link], the Global Covenant of Mayors issued a new funding call in Brussels.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">Multilateral banks including the EIB, EBRD and World Bank, appealed to states, pension funds and investment houses for $200m to fund developing cities and $600m for enhanced credit financing.</span></p>
<p class="p2"><span class="s3">This sum could then be leveraged up to $6bn, according to Cristiana Fragola, a director of the covenant.</span></p>
<p class="p4"><span class="s3">“If pension funds &#8211; most of which spend just one-two percent of their funds on climate action &#8211; were to allocate even a minor portion of their assets to this call, we could really move the needle substantially,” she told CHN.</span></p>

      function getCookie(cname) {
        var name = cname + "=";
        var decodedCookie = decodeURIComponent(document.cookie);
        var ca = decodedCookie.split(';');
        for(var i = 0; i <ca.length; i++) {
          var c = ca[i];
          while (c.charAt(0) == ' ') {
            c = c.substring(1);
          }
          if (c.indexOf(name) == 0) {
            return c.substring(name.length, c.length);
          }
        }
        return "";
      }
      function setCookie(cname, cvalue, exdays) {
        var d = new Date();
        d.setTime(d.getTime() + (exdays*24*60*60*1000));
        var expires = "expires="+ d.toUTCString();
        document.cookie = cname + "=" + cvalue + ";" + expires + ";path=/";
      }
