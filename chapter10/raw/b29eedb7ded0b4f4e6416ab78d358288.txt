    <h1 class="post-title">France: Mass protests against Macron's economic policies</h1>
    <p class="article-heading-des">Protesters march across the country to protest against government plans to change labour law, scrap social benefits. </p>
    <div class="article-heading-author-wrap">
      <div class="article-heading-author-img">
        <div class="article-heading-author-name">
          <div class="article-duration"><time class="timeagofunction" datetime="Thu Mar 22 2018 20:56:17 GMT+0000">22 Mar 2018 20:56 GMT</time></div>
        </div>

</div></div></div></div></div></div><div></div></div></div></div><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="row article-main-content-wrapper"><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="col-md-8 article-main-content"><div><div style=";border-style:none"><article id="main-article-block" class="main-article-block clear-fix"><div class="main-article-body"><figure class="main-article-mediaCaption"><div class="main-article-media"><img class="img-responsive main-article-media-img" src="/mritems/imagecache/mbdxxlarge/mritems/Images/2018/3/22/2221be45c0de43d5b0142997b4219794_18.jpg" alt="Workers are planning to strike two days out of every five throughout the months of April to June [Stephane Mahe/Reuters] " title="France: Mass protests against Macron's economic policies"></div><figcaption class="main-article-caption">Workers are planning to strike two days out of every five throughout the months of April to June [Stephane Mahe/Reuters] </figcaption></figure><div id="inpage-more-on-block" class="inpage-auto-embed hidden-xs"></div>
    <div class="article-p-wrapper" id="body-200771816342556199"><p class="speakable">Hundreds of thousands of public sector workers have marched across France to protest against the government's economic policies, including plans to reform public services and slash jobs.</p>
<p class="speakable">Staff from the national railway company, commonly known as SNCF, were joined on Thursday by employees of the national carrier Air France and other public servants as they took to the streets of the capital, Paris, and other major cities.</p>
<p class="speakable">The ministry of interior said 323,000 people took part in the nationwide protest, while the country's second largest trade union, the General Confederation of Labour, <span>put the number at</span>Â about half a million.</p>
<p>The demonstrators are protesting President Emmanuel Macron's plans to trim retirement benefits, overhaul unemployment insurance and allow SNCFâs competitors to enter the French market.</p>
<p>Al Jazeera's Natacha Butler, reporting from Paris, said the SNCF has been operating at a loss for a number of years.</p>
<p>"It has $50m of debt and the government says it must change to remain competitive, especially because under EU rules in 2020, the company will lose its monopoly," she said.</p>
<p>Edouard Philippe, France's prime minister, has previously said the government cannot not afford to sustain the same level of spending.</p>
<p>"The world is changing, the SNCF must change with,â he said in February. âFaced with competition, SNCF cannot remain the only company to recruit workers under a special status."</p>
<p>SNCF staff currently enjoy lifelong civil-servant-like contracts.</p>
<p>Public servants - including air traffic controllers - have also voiced their opposition to the governmentâs intention of introducing new ordinary work contract and recruiting more staff.</p>
<p>Workers are planning to hold protests two days out of every five through April to June.</p>
<p>This is not the first time workers have taken to the streets to demonstrate against Macron's labour plans.</p>
<p>Protests erupted last year to denounce legislation that would make employment contracts more flexible.</p></div>
