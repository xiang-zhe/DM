<h3  class="article__title">
  <a href="/news/tri-service/comment-why-we-shouldnt-snigger-north-korea" rel="bookmark"><span class="field field--name-title field--type-string field--label-hidden">Comment: Why We Shouldn&#039;t Snigger At North Korea </span>
</a>
</h3>


<div class="article__body">
  
</div>

  <a href="/news/tri-service/comment-why-we-shouldnt-snigger-north-korea" class="article__read-more">Read more</a>

</article>
</div>
      
      </div>

              <div class="field__item">  <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--rich-text paragraph--view-mode--default">
          
            <div class="basic-rich-text field field--name-field-rich-text field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>South Korea and the United States have announced that they will hold joint military drills, despite potential talks between President Trump and Kim Jong Un.</p>

<p>However, the drills are likely to be more low-key than past years ahead of two highly anticipated summits among the countries' leaders.</p>

<p>The Pentagon said the exercises will begin on April 1.</p>

<p>The announcement comes as Donald Trump prepares for a major political summit with Kim Jong Un.</p>

<p>The exercises, named “Foal Eagle and Key Resolve”, will last for four weeks, meaning they would end before Trump met with the North Korean leader.</p>

<p>They were postponed during the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, which saw rare co-operative steps between the rival Korean nations after months of confrontation over the North's weapons programmes.</p></div>
      
      </div>
</div>

            <div class="field field--name-field-caption field--type-string field--label-hidden field__item">U.S. Army and South Korean Army take part in Exercise Warrior Strike IX in South Korea in December</div>
      </figcaption>
</figure>
</div>
              <div class="field__item">  <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--rich-text paragraph--view-mode--default">
          
            <div class="basic-rich-text field field--name-field-rich-text field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><p>A Pentagon spokesman said:</p>

<p>"Secretary of Defense James N. Mattis and the Republic of Korea Minister of National Defense Song Young-moo have agreed to resume the annual combined exercises including Foal Eagle and Key Resolve which were de-conflicted with the schedule of the Olympic Games.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>“The exercises are expected to resume April 1, 2018, at a scale similar to that of the previous years.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>"The United Nations Command has notified the Korean People's Army on the schedule as well as the defensive nature of the annual exercises."</p>

<p>North Korea is known to view the exercises held between the US and South Korea as a form of provocation, and has used them as justification for nuclear testing in the past.</p>

<p>The exercises begin with Foal Eagle, a field training drill that will last about four weeks, compared with its typical two-month run.</p>

<p>The other drill, known as Key Resolve, is a computer-simulated command post exercise and is scheduled to start around the middle of April for a usual two-week run, a South Korean official said.</p>

<p>Choi Kang, vice president of Seoul's Asan Institute for Policy Studie said: </p>

<blockquote>
<p>"These are low-key drills. Now it's a dialogue mode so they are trying to keep pace with that."</p>
</blockquote></div>
