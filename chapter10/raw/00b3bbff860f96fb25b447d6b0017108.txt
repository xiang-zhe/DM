<h3>Russia launches large-scale military exercises on border with Ukraine</h3>


            <div class="metadata">
                <div class="meta well well-sm">
    <i class="icon-calendar"></i>&nbsp;Tuesday, March 20, 2018 9:00:46 AM
</div>
<div><span></span></div>

            </div>
    </header>
    

<section class="media-library-picker-field media-library-picker-field-featured-image ">
    
        <div>
                    <a href="/russia-launches-large-scale-military-exercises-on-border-with-ukraine">
                        
<article class="media-item image">
    <header>
        
    </header>
    

<a href="">
    <img class="img-responsive img-responsive-pad20" alt="" src="/Media/Default/_Profiles/4f777d2b/abbfa561/artillery.jpg?v=636209481073548939" />
</a>




</article>
                    </a>

</div>
        </section>

<p>On Monday, March 19, in various areas of Russia, and also in the annexed Crimea, large-scale artillery exercises of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation began, reported the press service of the Southern Military District of Russia.</p>
<p>About 8,000 servicemen and nearly 1,400 pieces of military equipment are involved in the maneuvers, among them the Tornado, Grad and Uragan multiple launch rocket systems; Khosta, Msta-S and Akatsiya self-propelled artillery mounts; and self-propelled Khrizantema anti-tank missile systems.</p>
<p>During the exercises, the Russian military will also work on a &ldquo;combined&rdquo; method of transferring military equipment.</p>
