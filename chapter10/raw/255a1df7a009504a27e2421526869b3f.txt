		<div>
			
<p>Soon the purveyors of official Britishness will likely be Dutch.</p>
<p>The British company De La Rue, which has made the UK&rsquo;s passports for the past nine years, has lost the bid to manufacture the kingdom&rsquo;s redesigned post-Brexit passport. Gemalto, a Dutch company with headquarters outside Paris, in Singapore, and in Austin, Texas, has <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/5869560/home-office-accused-of-national-humiliation-after-deciding-new-blue-british-passports-will-be-made-in-france/">reportedly won</a> the contract, though the British home office has declined to confirm.</p>
<p>The UK announced in December that, free from the shackles of the EU&rsquo;s citizenship standards, it would return to the iconic dark-blue British passport. (It had switched to burgundy in 1988.) The new passports will be issued in 2019, after the UK&rsquo;s official exit from the EU. The immigration office <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/news/blue-uk-passport-to-return-after-eu-exit">called the return to blue</a> &ldquo;a move to symbolise our national identity,&rdquo; a line that looks all the more spurious in light of word of the new contract.</p>
<div class="inline-ad" id="inline-1235414-1"></div><p>Without alluding to the winning company, a home office spokesperson said in an emailed statement that &ldquo;the preferred bidder has been selected following a rigorous, fair and open competition.&rdquo; The department points out that since 2009, it hasn&rsquo;t been a requirement for the UK passports to be manufactured domestically, and that 20% of its blank passport books are currently made in wider Europe. The new contract&mdash;reportedly <a href="https://www.ft.com/content/e1578076-2d5e-11e8-a34a-7e7563b0b0f4">worth &pound;490 million</a> (about $690 million) over 10 years&mdash;will create 70 new jobs in the UK and save taxpayers &pound;120 million over the length of the contract, adds the home office.</p>
<p>Gemalto, a public company registered in the Netherlands, would not confirm that it has won the contract. &ldquo;As the process is still ongoing and the terms of engagement are confidential, we cannot make any further comment on it at this stage,&rdquo; it said.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, De La Rue is not going quietly. &ldquo;We are deeply disappointed that the contract to manufacture the British passport will be awarded to a French company,&rdquo; the company said in an email, adding, &ldquo;This shows a serious lack of commitment to British business from the UK government.&rdquo; On its site, De La Rue says it will consider appealing the decision. De La Rue makes passports for 40 countries and printed the new &pound;5 note, as well as the &pound;10 note, which features the face of Jane Austen.</p>
