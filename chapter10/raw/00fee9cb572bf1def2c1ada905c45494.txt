                            <p class="incut">The editorial office was &quot;surprised and disappointed&quot; by the decision of the commission, which &quot;actually refused to analyze the charges&quot; and &quot;turned the session into a trial of the victims.&quot;</p>
                                    </aside>
            <p><p>
 <i>Kommersant</i> Publishing House says that it refuses to work with MP Leonid Slutsky and the State Duma ethics commission, which examined the complaints of two journalists over sexual harassment committed by the deputy and found no violations in his behavior.
</p>
<p>
	 The editorial office was "surprised and disappointed" by the decision of the commission, which "actually refused to analyze the charges" and "turned the session into a trial of the victims."
</p>
<p>
	 "We hope that there is no point in this story. But for our part, we put an end to the relationship with Deputy Slutsky as a newsmaker and ethics committee, as we adhere to other ethical views," the editorial office of <i>Kommersant</i> states.
</p>
<p>
	 Later, the <i>Dozhd</i> TV channel announced that it would suspend&nbsp;its interaction with the State Duma, "while Speaker Vyacheslav Volodin and leader of the <i>LDPR</i> faction Vladimir Zhirinovsky will not put things right." The TV channel also insists on reviewing the commission's decision and "conducting a real investigation."
</p>
<p>
	 We consider the approach of commission members, for whom corporate ethics are more important than justice, as destructive.
</p>
<p>
	 Previously, <i>RBC</i> media holding stated that it would recall all journalists working in the State Duma in disagreement with the decision of the ethics commission. "The ethics commission, in fact, recognized the possibility of sexual harassment against journalists as a norm," the management of the editorial office said.
</p>
<p>
	 On March 21, the ethics commission of the State Duma considered the complaints of Farida Rustamova, the journalist of the <i>BBC Russian Service</i>, and Daria Zhuk, the producer of <i>Dozhd</i>, over sexual harassment committed by MP Leonid Slutsky and did not find any "violations of behavioral norms."
</p></p>
