    <h1 class="post-title">UNSC: 13 million people need humanitarian aid in DR Congo</h1>
    <p class="article-heading-des">Citing ongoing political turmoil, malnutrition, and a cholera outbreak, UN says millions need aid in the DRC.</p>
    <div class="article-heading-author-wrap">
      <div class="article-heading-author-img">
        <div class="article-heading-author-name">
          <div class="article-duration"><time class="timeagofunction" datetime="Mon Mar 19 2018 20:48:09 GMT+0000">19 Mar 2018 20:48 GMT</time></div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="article-readToMe-share-block" id="article-top-share-icon">

</div></div></div></div></div></div><div></div></div></div></div><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="row article-main-content-wrapper"><div><div><div><div style="width:100%"><div><div class="col-md-8 article-main-content"><div><div style=";border-style:none"><article id="main-article-block" class="main-article-block clear-fix"><div class="main-article-body"><figure class="main-article-mediaCaption"><div class="main-article-media"><img class="img-responsive main-article-media-img" src="/mritems/imagecache/mbdxxlarge/mritems/Images/2018/3/19/c34b9dfdedd7418db9bbe59c5797e7b6_18.jpg" alt="A Congolese soldier carries a box of ammunition near the town of Kimbau, North Kivu province [File: Reuters]" title="UNSC: 13 million people need humanitarian aid in DR Congo"></div><figcaption class="main-article-caption">A Congolese soldier carries a box of ammunition near the town of Kimbau, North Kivu province [File: Reuters]</figcaption></figure><div id="inpage-more-on-block" class="inpage-auto-embed hidden-xs"></div>
    <div class="article-p-wrapper" id="body-200771816342556199"><p class="speakable">Humanitarian needs caused by conflict in the Democratic Republic of Congo (<a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/topics/country/posting_under_country.html">DRC</a>) have doubled over the last year, t<span>he UN Security Council said</span>.</p>
<p class="speakable">Speaking on Monday, the UN's Under-Secretary-General Mark Lowcock said 13 million people are affected by internal conflict gripping the country and require <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/topics/categories/humanitarian-crises.html">humanitarian</a> assistance.</p>
<p class="speakable">"More than 4.6 million children are acutely malnourished, including 2.2 million suffering from severe acute malnutrition," Lowcock said.</p>
<p>"We're seeing mushrooming epidemics including the worst outbreak of cholera in 15 years. There's also an epidemic of sexual violence - most of it unreported and unaddressed - and much of it against children."</p>
<p>Fighting in the central African country has much to do with the long-delayed vote to replace President <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/programmes/insidestory/2018/01/drc-president-joseph-kabila-step-180101194648615.html">Joseph Kabila</a>.</p>
<p>While elections were initially set for November 2016, they were pushed to December 2018. But the electoral commission has since said voting may not be possible until April 2019.</p>
<p>In the meantime, political opposition has been building up and a report released by the UN on Monday said at least 47 people were killed in the past year in demonstration-related violence.</p>
<h2><strong>Various warlords</strong></h2>
<p>Speaking from the UN headquarters in New York City, Al Jazeera's Mike Hanna said the UN Security Council is drawing attention to the <a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2018/02/thousands-flee-atrocities-south-eastern-drc-180220143919759.html">crisis in DRC</a> - one that involves many layers.</p>
<p>"There's the ongoing political uncertainty with a president who has overstayed his constitutional term in office," Hanna said.</p>
<p>"There's been ongoing conflict particularly in the north of the country where there are rebel groups still operating. There are also military groups associated with various warlords in the particular region."</p>
<p>This has resulted in a massive amount of internally displaced people, Hanna said.</p>
<p>The UN has scheduled a donor's conference on April 13, where Lowcock said $1.7bn in aid is needed for this year. To date, only four percent of the funds needed for 2018 has been received by the UN.</p>
<p>"Underfunding is the largest single impediment to the humanitarian response in the DRC," Lowcock said, adding the crisis will continue to escalate if there is no successful political transition and a halt to the violence.</p></div>
