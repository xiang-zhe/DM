						<p>The World Trade Organisation (WTO) has suggested that the <a href="https://9to5mac.com/guides/iphone/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">iPhone</a> plays a significant role in skewing the apparent US trade deficit with <a href="https://9to5mac.com/guides/china/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">China</a>.</p>
<p>On the surface, the deficit appears to be $375B, with the iPhone 7 series alone accounting for around 4.4% of that, but the true figure is said to be far lower &#8230;</p>
<p><span id="more-525206"></span>
	<ins class="adsbygoogle author_ad" style="display:block;" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2445248216782983" data-ad-slot="9911472330"></ins>
<script type="text/javascript">(adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});</script>
		
<div class="rdc9ab3ac1 rd08edaefe"><div class="rd64ac7847"><img src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" class="rd1b2041c1"/></div></div><div class="rdd6b9c4bd rd591251ac"><div class="rd64ac7847"><img src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" class="rd1b2041c1"/></div></div><div class="rd4f9c794b rd5df27b29"><div class="rd64ac7847"><div class="rd553fc571 rd3def3d6e"><div class="rd64ac7847" data-rd40b06423="https://go.nordvpn.net/aff_c?offer_id=15&#038;aff_id=1788&#038;url_id=902"><img src="https://9to5mac.files.wordpress.com/2013/03/screen-shot-2017-03-30-at-14-48-26.png?resize=300%2C250&#038;quality=82&#038;strip=all"/><h3>NordVPN</h3></div></div></div></div><div class="rd7ac6825e rd0f4b0a98"><div class="rd64ac7847"><img src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" class="rd1b2041c1"/></div></div><div class="rd3391d111 rd2f20e4a1"><div class="rd64ac7847"><img src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" class="rd1b2041c1"/></div></div></p>
<p><a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-trade-china-apple/designed-in-california-made-in-china-how-the-iphone-skews-u-s-trade-deficit-idUSKBN1GX1GZ?feedType=RSS&amp;feedName=technologyNews&amp;utm_source=feedburner&amp;utm_medium=feed&amp;utm_campaign=Feed%3A+reuters%2FtechnologyNews+%28Reuters+Technology+News%29" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><em>Reuters</em></a> reports that because iPhones are assembled in China, the headline numbers count almost the entire manufacturing cost of an iPhone, while in reality very little of that money is spent in China.</p>
<blockquote>
<p class="">Take a look at the iPhone X. IHS Markit estimates its components cost a total of $370.25. Of that, $110 goes to Samsung Electronics in South Korea for supplying displays. Another $44.45 goes to Japan’s Toshiba Corp and South Korea’s SK Hynix for memory chips.</p>
<p class="">Other suppliers from Taiwan, the United States and Europe also take their portion, while assembly, done by contract manufacturers in China like Foxconn, represents only an estimated 3 to 6 percent of the manufacturing cost.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Other economists agree.</p>
<blockquote>
<p class="">“With an iPhone, where China is just the final assembler, most of the value (contributed by China) is just the labor rather than the components themselves,” said John Wu, an economic analyst with a U.S.-based think tank, the Information &amp; Innovation Foundation.</p>
<p class="">Louis Kuijs, head of Asia economics research at Oxford Economics [added] that in value added terms, the U.S. trade deficit with China was only $239 billion last year, 36 percent lower than the headline number.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The analysis comes at a time when some of the largest US companies are calling for Trump to back-track on his threatened import tariffs on Chinese goods to avoid catastrophic damage to the economy.</p>
<blockquote>
<p class="">Forty-five U.S. trade associations representing some of the largest U.S. companies urged the president on Sunday not to impose tariffs on China, warning it would be “particularly harmful” to the U.S. economy and consumers [&#8230;]</p>
<p class="">A 10 percent tariff levied on Chinese electronics imports would slow the growth of U.S. output by $163 billion over the next 10 years, and a 25 percent tariff would slow output by $332 billion, according to the Information Technology &amp; Innovation Foundation.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Analysts recently suggested that <a href="https://9to5mac.com/2018/03/07/trade-war-china-apple/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Apple products</a> could even be caught in the import tariff net, depending on how imports are defined.</p>
