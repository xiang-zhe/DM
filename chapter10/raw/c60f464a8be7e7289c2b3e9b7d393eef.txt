<div class="article-body-text component  version-2">
<div class="component-content">
<p><span class="m_first-letter m_first-letter--flagged">B</span>MW has been raided by German authorities investigating the luxury car maker over claims it cheated pollution controls.</p>
<p>Prosecutors visited the companys landmark Munich base as well as a satellite site in Austria in relation to more than 11,000 cars which are thought to have passed emissions tests because they were fitted with software which allowed them to recognise when they were being checked.</p>
<p>These so-called defeat devices identify when emissions are being checked and turn on pollution controls in diesel engines which are not employed in normal driving.</p>
<p>This system means cars pump out far higher levels of pollution when on the roads when compared with during testing procedures.</p>
<p>In 2015 Volkswagenadmitted that 11m of its cars worldwide were fitted with similar devices,<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/business/2017/09/01/two-years-dieselgate-volkswagen-launches-diesel-scrappage-scheme/"> leading to the dieselgate scandal </a>which saw the companys share price crash and led to the German manufacturer paying tens of billions of euros in costs, compensation and fines.</p>

<p><span class="m_first-letter m_first-letter--flagged">G</span>erman prosecutors confirmed they had opened an investigation into BMW on suspicion of fraud affecting 11,400 cars, with the probe drawing in both managers and staff at the company.</p>
<p>The raids come at a sensitive time for BMW, with its executives due to face the worlds media on Wednesday for its annual results press conference.</p>

<p><span class="m_first-letter m_first-letter--flagged">B</span>MW confirmed the raids, saying they were related to a software update mistakenly allocated to high end 750d and 550d models.</p>
<p>The company said the problem was discovered during internal tests and the cars have been recalled for an update.</p>
<p>An internal investigation has been launched and BMW said it takes the situation very seriously and has a significant interest in the circumstances being fully explained.</p>
<p>It added: BMW continues to assume that the situation was caused by an incorrect allocation of the software and does not represent a deliberate attempt to manipulate exhaust emissions.</p>

<p>This is not the first time BMW has been raided by over alleged misdeeds.<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/business/2017/10/20/anti-trust-watchdog-searches-bmws-hq/"> In October EU prosecutors searched the companys headquarters as part of a wider probe into claims the company, along with peers Daimler and VW</a>, colluded on technical standards to allow them to fix prices, though no action has so far been taken.</p>
